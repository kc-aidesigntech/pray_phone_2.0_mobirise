Main components of
a web design portfolio

Homepage

A brief introduction about yourself or your agency, highlighting your expertise in web design and your unique selling points.

About Page

A detailed bio that shares your background, education, and experience in web design. Describe your approach to web design, your creative process, and what inspires your work.

Projects Page

Visually appealing thumbnails for each project that entice visitors to click and learn more. A summary of the project’s objectives, your role, and the client.

Services Page

Clearly list the services you offer, such as web design, UI/UX design, responsive design, e-commerce solutions, and maintenance.

Testimonials

Display positive feedback from clients to build trust and showcase your reliability and expertise. Incorporate quotes from clients within your case studies to add authenticity.

Contact Page

A simple and user-friendly contact form for inquiries. Provide your email address, phone number, and physical address if applicable.

FAQs when putting together
a web design portfolio

What should I include in my web design portfolio?

Your web design portfolio should include a variety of your best work, showcasing different styles and types of projects. Include detailed case studies with project goals, your design process, and the results achieved.

How many projects should I feature in my portfolio?

Aim to feature 5-10 high-quality projects. It's better to have a few outstanding examples than to overwhelm potential clients with too many mediocre ones. Quality over quantity is key.

Should I include personal projects or only client work?

Both are valuable. Client work demonstrates your ability to meet client needs and work within constraints, while personal projects can showcase your creativity, passion, and skills in areas you might not get to explore in client work.

How do I present each project in my portfolio?

For each project, include a brief overview, your role, the challenges faced, the design process, and the outcomes. Use visuals like screenshots, wireframes, and prototypes to illustrate your points. If possible, include metrics that demonstrate the project's success.
